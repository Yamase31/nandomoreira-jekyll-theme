Thursday, April 21st, 2016

Grace in class said that she didn’t think that Obama was an internationalist and I disagree with her, I think that Obama is an internationalist. Reading the atlantic piece and from watching the news, I got the sense that Obama has a crazy travel schedule. While Goldberg was with Obama, Obama was on a crazy travel schedule that took him to Turkey, Malaysia, and Indonesia just to name a few countries. He was there for the sole reason of strengthening economic and political ties. He also spent the last six months working on the Trans-Pacific Partnership which is a trade agreement between 12 pacific rim countries signed on February 4th, 2016. How can you say that Obama is not an internationalist when he spends significant time traveling to strengthen ties and building the framework of international trade agreements.

Thursday, April 28th, 2016

Adam in class said that he thinks that ISIS is trying to get the US to react in a way that would make them look bad and in a way that ISIS could use to try and further their narrative. Part of ISIS’s narrative is that the US and the West is occupying their lands and trying to convert them away from Christianity. ISIS wants the US to react with force and with troops on the ground after ISIS has perpetrated attacks in the western world such as the paris attacks or the Brussels attacks. After the US has reacted with force, they can say, “look at this western country who is attacking us” and then try to make the US look like the bad guy to gain support and followers. The US, just as Mr. Mann said, should never react with a outright strong force and should react with a smaller number of troops that are very effective, such as special operation advisors. The US cannot commit the same mistake that it made during the Iraq and Afghanistan war again.

Tuesday, May 3rd, 2016

Molly said something that I personally thought was ridiculous in class. As we were talking about if the US should kill the head of ISIS, she said that she didn’t think it was a good idea because it would bring the world one step closer to the apocalypse. ISIS says that once the 12th caliph has been killed (Baghdadi is the 8th), the “end of days” or “apocalypse” will occur. First off, the fact that ISIS and this student thinks that the death of one person will bring the “end of days” is ridiculous. ISIS doesn’t have the power to make a real dent in modern society and even if they tried, the US military would put an end to it. Secondly, the fact that this student thinks that the US military should make its decisions based off one ancient story in the Quran is risible. The US military makes its decisions based off data and knowledge, not off scripture. While I personally think that there are many arguments to support both sides of killing or not killing Baghdadi, this reason should not be even considered.
